A good place to start with a plan is to create an overview of what you want your project to achieve. One potential risk of a digital project is that the platform you are writing for could update, causing the written code to become outdated and stop working, this becomes more likely as the project gets bigger. Another potential risk is that you could be working in a team and one or more of your associates could have another, more lucrative opportunity arise and abandon the project. Another risk is that your project may not be given the necessary resources, or it might have them to start and then the business might reallocate these resources to another task they deem to be a higher priority. > Outcome 1.5

When selecting what IT systems and software to use it is important to know your objective, you need to know what you need to know what you want the software to do for you, to help you accurately select the right tool. If you are selecting software for a business to use it could be valuable to bring heads of departments onboard and put together a list of all the requirements the company has for the software. It is also important to weigh up the functional requirements with the usability requirements, you might be able to find software that has all the required functionality, but the usability is so low that only the people in the IT department are comfortable to use it. On the other hand, there might be a software that is very usable, but only does half of the necessary things. It is crucial to find a balance. It is also important to think about longevity compared to budget and cost. There’s no point spending more than the company can afford to on a piece of software which is going to become obsolete within 2 years. Once these have all been decided the most plausible next step would be to create a long list within a spread sheet. This list should include relevant software packages, though there is no point listing the pros/cons of these softwares as there is many comparison sites you can use for this. At this point, it’s easiest to rule out softwares that are unsuitable for whatever reason. Once you have narrowed the list down to the ones that tick all the fundamental criteria and are within budget, it is time to begin testing them with demos and trials. A good way to effectively judge the different softwares whilst you trial them, is to create a requirements matrix.

> Outcome 1.7

Legal guidelines that impact digital projects involve the right to access - any individual has the right to request access to all their personal data held by the company, and to ask how their data is being used by the company. The company must facilitate the individuals request, free of charge, or risk being taken to court over breaching GDPR. Individuals also hold the right to be forgotten, if consumers are no longer customers or if they revoke their consent from the company, then they have the right to have their data deleted. Individuals also have the right to data portability - this means, upon request, the company must transfer their data to another service provider, and it must be sent in a commonly used and machine readable format.

> Outcome 1.5

Copyright is a law regarding to intellectual property. Intellectual property means that people have ownership over the things they create, like how something psychical can be owned. Copyright infringement happens when a person doesn’t have permission from the creator but they still show the copywriter thing to the public or copy it. This can affect images you might want to use for your website, if it’s copyrighted you need the permission of the individual or company that owns the image to be able to put it on your website. If you take your website live and have copyrighted images that you don’t have permission to use, this is copyright infringement and you could get sued. Also, you would need to have permission from the author if you wanted to include a section from a copyrighted book on your website.

> Outcome 1.6

One access issue that needs to be taken into account when creating a website is bad vision, many people don’t have the best eye sight and can find it hard to read text if it’s a similar colour to whatever is behind it. It is important to remember this when choosing colours, and make sure it’s not similar. Another access issue could be browser type, if your website works on Chrome but not on IE/Safari/Firefox you are cutting out a lot of potential visitors. It is vital to make sure your site works as intended on all browsers. It is also important to add the alternative text to images, so if a blind person visits your site using a text to speech add on, they will hear what the pictures are that they can’t see.
